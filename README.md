# UI-UX-Experiment-5
## Aim
To analyze UX project scenarios, identify user problems, select appropriate research methods, and create user personas to better understand target users and their needs.

# Scenario 1: Health & Fitness Tracking Mobile App

## 1. Problem Statements

### Identified User Pain Points
- Users forget to track daily health activities.
- Difficulty understanding personal health data.
- Lack of motivation to maintain healthy habits.

### UX Problem Statements

1. **Busy professionals** need a way to **track their daily health activities quickly** because **they have limited time and complex health apps**, which results in **inconsistent health monitoring**.

2. **Health-conscious users** need a way to **understand their fitness progress easily** because **data in many health apps is difficult to interpret**, which results in **reduced motivation to continue healthy routines**.

## 2. Research Methods

### Selected Methods

**1. User Interviews**
- Helps understand users' daily health habits.
- Provides deep insights into motivations and challenges.

**2. Surveys**
- Allows collecting data from many users quickly.
- Helps identify common health tracking behaviors.

**Justification**

User interviews provide **qualitative insights** into personal health routines and barriers, while surveys provide **quantitative data** about user habits and preferences. Combining both methods helps build a comprehensive understanding of user needs.

# Scenario 2: E-Commerce Shopping Website

## 1. Problem Statements

### Identified User Pain Points
- Users find it difficult to discover relevant products.
- Long checkout processes cause cart abandonment.
- Lack of clear product information.

### UX Problem Statements

1. **Online shoppers** need a way to **find relevant products quickly** because **large product catalogs are difficult to navigate**, which results in **frustration and abandoned searches**.

2. **Customers** need a way to **complete purchases quickly** because **complex checkout processes require too many steps**, which results in **cart abandonment and lost sales**.

## 2. Research Methods

### Selected Methods

**1. Usability Testing**
- Observes how users interact with the website.
- Identifies navigation and checkout issues.

**2. Online Surveys**
- Collect feedback on shopping experiences.
- Identify common frustrations and preferences.

**Justification**

Usability testing helps identify **interaction problems in real scenarios**, while surveys provide **user opinions and feedback from a larger audience**, making the research more reliable.


## 3. User Personas

### Persona 1

**Name:** Ananya Patel  
**Age:** 24  
**Occupation:** College Student  
**Location:** Mumbai  

**Demographics**
- Shops online frequently
- Uses mobile devices for shopping

**Goals**
- Find trendy products quickly
- Compare prices easily

**Behaviors**
- Browses multiple websites before purchasing
- Relies on product reviews

**Pain Points**
- Too many product options
- Difficult product filtering

**Motivation**
- Convenience and better deals

### Persona 2

**Name:** Vikram Singh  
**Age:** 38  
**Occupation:** Business Owner  
**Location:** Delhi  

**Demographics**
- Busy professional
- Prefers efficient online shopping

**Goals**
- Purchase products quickly
- Secure and simple checkout process

**Behaviors**
- Shops during limited free time
- Prefers trusted platforms

**Pain Points**
- Long checkout forms
- Unclear delivery information

**Motivation**
- Save time and ensure reliable purchases
